Ex-Dividend Date vs. Payment Date – The 30-Second Refresher

📅 Declaration date: The company announces the dividend amount and dates.

⏰ Ex-dividend date: The cutoff. You must own shares before this date to get paid. Buy on or after it, and the dividend goes to the seller.

📋 Record date: Usually one business day after the ex-date — the day the company checks its shareholder list.

💰 Payment date: The day cash actually hits your account.

The ex-dividend date is the only one that changes your behavior. Every calendar below puts it front and center — that's a minimum requirement to make this list.

FAQ: Quick Answers on Dividend Calendars

What's the best free dividend calendar in 2026?

Stock Analysis. Full ex-date and payout coverage for stocks and ETFs, searchable, fast, and no signup required.

What's the difference between the ex-dividend date and the payment date?

The ex-dividend date is the ownership cutoff — buy before it or you don't get paid. The payment date is when the cash actually arrives, usually a few weeks later.

Can a dividend calendar alert me before ex-dividend dates?

Yes — Snowball Analytics and DivTracker both send notifications before ex-dates and payment dates on your synced holdings.

Do dividend calendars cover ETFs too?

Most do, but quality varies. Stock Analysis and Nasdaq both list ETF distribution ex-dates and amounts alongside individual stocks.
